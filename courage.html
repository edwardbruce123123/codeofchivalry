Courage

Learn about the virtue of Courage in chivalry.

Courage was one of the most revered virtues in the Code of Chivalry. Courage symbolizes a knight's willingness to confront fear, danger, and adversity, both on and off the battlefield. Courage isn't just about physical bravery, throwing yourself into danger. It is also about moral strength; phycological strength, the ability to make difficult decisions with honour, even if its a sacrifice.

Knights were also expected to protect the helpless, defend their lord and land, and uphold justice. Courage also meant standing firm during injustice. showing physical and mental strength when present with problems. All while keeping to their beliefs and ideas of the knightly code.

A knights courage was a balance between valour in battle, and integrity in daily life. Representing the perfect chivalrous knight through both character and action. It was because of this strict and integral way of life that gives knights their reputation.

This is Sir William Marshal, who is often regarded as one of the greatest knight of the medieval period. Known as "the greatest knight who ever lived," his life was judged by both battlefield valour and abiding loyalty. Marshal served under many notable English kings, including Henry II, Richard the Lionheart and King John.

His courage was demonstrated not only in battles and tournaments, but also in his defence of the English throne during danger. His reputation for unwavering honour and bravery made him a legendary figure in medieval lore, Showing the ideals of the Code Of Chivalry.
